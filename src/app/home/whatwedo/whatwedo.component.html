What We Do

To increase the ability of more than 800 grassroots organisations, Indian Social Responsibility Network has periodically organised a number of seminars and training programmes. It will aid our extended network of partners and enable them to be structured into efficient, well-managed organisations that make best use of their people and financial resources to preserve business sustainability. The Training Program strives to empower NGOs by giving effective strategies, cutting-edge tools, as well as the specialised skills and techniques required to grow their potential. This will enable the organisations to operate effectively both internally and towards the community they serve. Our devoted team's current effort on various theme concepts has allowed us to concentrate on four key areas: agriculture, education, health, and capacity building. Through motivated ground team members and a group of volunteers, our team leads have covered the last mile in each of these diﬀerent thematic domains.

Advocacy

ISRN has organised a number of programs that require participants to learn about concerned government policies and laws along with applied policy and management analysis. In a globalised world that is constantly evolving, we need citizens in all sectors namely government organisations, private companies and those working at the ground level who understand the issues in sustainability, and have the practical skills and knowledge pertaining to the existing and upcoming policies to deal with issues effectively. A major aspect of advocacy programs is ISRN's disposition to engage directly with stakeholders in practical efforts to improve the scenario on the ground. Our efforts involve introducing innovative approaches to address real-world problems.

Health

ISRN has been a great contributor in providing general and specialised health care services at door-step and making health services accessible to vulnerable masses. It has organised vaccination drives to fight Covid-19 virus, regularly functioning health centres encouraging the medical/para-medical staff to work more efficiently; it also has provided free, timely, preventive, curative and promotive healthcare facilities and health camps check-ups to the target population living with limited access to dispensaries and government hospitals.

Need

Assessment

Indian Social Responsibility Network along with the team of Centre for Digital Financial Inclusion initiated a village development plan in the Tarapur Village of Neemuch district and Behpur village of Mandsaur district in Madhya Pradesh. A survey for need assessment was carried out to develop SAGY Gram Panchayats as model Gram Panchayats so that all the relevant government schemes, projects and resources are executed properly. The data collection in the gram panchayat was carried out through an app called KoBoCollect. The app has two different forms, one for data from households and another for data about the villages.
On the basis of the data, CDFI and ISRN will prepare the VDP with the involvement of village panchayat members and village functionaries. CDFI and ISRN will also plan for the resource mobilisation, project planning, implementation strategy and facilitation towards fulfilling the needs of VDP.

ISRN

Network

ISRN Membership is open to Corporate and VOs. The memberships bring the advantages of knowledge, networking and resources that help Corporates meet their CSR objectives and statutory obligations under companies that act easily and efficiently, while making resources available to VOs.

Eligibility Criteria:

To be eligible to apply for the membership, your organisation needs to meet either of the following requirements: VOs - Societies Registration Act, 1860, Indian Trusts Act, 1882, The Charitable and Religious Trusts Act, 1920. Corporate - Company registered under the Companies Act, 1956 or Companies Act, 2013
