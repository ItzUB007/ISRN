Meghalaya and Nagaland

Background on COVID19 vaccination

COVID-19 vaccination program in India is one of the largest vaccination programs globally. Under the program a total of 1.89 billion vaccines have been administered across the country. With increase in vaccine uptake, the positivity rate has declined over the past few weeks. Around 62.3% people are fully vaccinated, 72.7% have received at least one dose and 2% have received booster doses. But with this the vaccination program has slowed down despite availability of doses. Major reasons identified are relaxation in various restrictions imposed by the government, reduction in positive cases and lack of interest in targeted population vaccination.
In the state of Nagaland, a total of 15.82 lakh doses were administered, including 2,444 children between 12-14 years by 1st April 2022. As per a report of DHFW, 65% of eligible targeted population have received first dose and 52% have received second dose. The project team has learned ground challenges and high reluctance due to religious beliefs amongst the population, there is huge population inflation as per the 2011 census and actual headcount available in the district. In the state of Meghalaya as per government dashboard, around 24 lakh doses have been administered including HCW, FLW and eligible citizens. More than 62% of the eligible population have received the first dose and more than 41% have received the second dose. Both the states have geographical and social challenges in order to reach maximum vaccination coverage. One of the shared challenges amongst both the states districts are the number of reduced/non-functional COVID 19 vaccination centers. Both the states had been doing fairly well in terms of vaccination coverage despite various pre-existing challenges like vaccine hesitancy, inaccessible geographies, myths and misinformation etc. Despite several attempts, few districts have been identified in both the states which are far behind in terms of vaccination coverage compared to state average. ISRN in collaboration with under the M-RITE project, has identified districts with lower vaccination rates. These districts have been further classified based into high and low priority districts based on the strength of interventions required in these districts. Based on the initial learning in a few districts, the team has identified various new districts in both the states where the vaccination program can be strengthened.

Goals and Objectives of the proposal

To increase the demand, distribution and uptake of COVID 19 vaccination among all GoI approved cohorts, particularly for the vulnerable and marginalized populations.

Objective
  • To support state and district Health departments in micro planning, and rapidly increasing vaccination programs to achieve maximum coverage.
  • To vaccinate all eligible unvaccinated beneficiaries in the allotted districts in the intervention areas.
  • To stimulate demand generation and community mobilization activities for COVID 19 vaccination uptake.
  • To ensure equitable distribution and focus on the last-mile reach of COVID-19 vaccination for all government approved cohorts, including high priority groups and left out targeted population

Geographical Area

In the state of Meghalaya, there are 4 districts where ISRN is currently implementing the M-RITE COVID- 19 vaccination program, which will be further sustained with increased targets for coverage. The new 3 districts East Khasi Hills, West Garo Hills and West Jaintia Hills, where the 2nd and and Precautionary doses coverage is low, so proposed to be added to the existing districts. Moreover, there are various natural challenges that the communities are facing in the hilly terrain. In Nagaland, ISRN is currently implementing M-RITE COVID-19 Vaccination program in 6 districts, where ISRN will provide support to amplify the Government vaccinations in addition to the 4 newly proposed districts.

Awareness Generation

ISRN took steps to create a mass level of awareness through Social Media platforms regarding Covid-19. In times when people are doubtful and afraid, it’s really important to project correct and complete information among the masses from authentic sources like Govt. websites. The organization urged to stay at home and follow the guidelines through Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, and Email as well. ISRN also mobilized its member organizations across the country to take up initiatives regarding Awareness Generation, Distributing Essential items, Preventive kits, and Surveillance to reach out to the maximum number of people.

Distribution of Food, Essentials, and Preventive Kit

The lockdown impacted every group of society however, the most affected were the labourers, daily wage earners, and migrant workers who faced more challenges in terms of basic amenities in such tough times. ISRN distributed groceries, food, dry ration, and sanitization kits to the needy ones especially migrant workers in slum and industrial areas of Ghaziabad and Greater Noida. 1200 people have been helped so far through this initiative. Frontline functionaries such as doctors and health workers, police, etc are working round the clock risking their lives to fight the virus and save lives. The organization has taken initiative for the protection of health workers by supplying 50 PPE kits to KMGC. ISRN also distributed hot water flasks and tiffin to ensure their good health.

Dialogues with Dr. Vinay Sahasrabuddhe

In order to understand the ground realities of the varied geography of India, ISRN organized several Dialogues with Dr. Vinay Sahasrabuddhe where 150 Voluntary Organizations of four states namely, Bihar, Uttar Pradesh, North-East, and Uttrakhand participated to discuss the challenges, response, and solutions of Covid-19.

Voluntary Reserve Force (VRF)

With an objective to support the frontline functionaries and others who are working round the clock to control the pandemic, Indian Social Responsibility Network (ISRN) has taken an initiative of establishing a Volunteer Reserve Force (VRF). The idea behind the initiative is to support the emergency functionaries and others who are working round the clock to control the pandemic. Some creatives of the campaign are attached with the mail for your reference. Till now 5000 volunteers from 31 States and UT’s have registered. These volunteers belong from different backgrounds like health, IT, security, media, banking and finance, supply chains, civil servants, civil defense, drivers, fire fighters, students, etc. who are eager to serve the people of the nation with their respective set of skills. In these critical times, VRF data can be utilized to help where manpower support is required, these volunteers can be placed to nearby areas of their residing to combat COVID-19. Training will have to be provided to these volunteers in safety measures and the work to be assigned to them, by the placement agency, keeping the current situation in mind.

Webinar

ISRN is part of a Global COVID-19 Network and is organizing a webinar (in 6 sessions) in collaboration with Sambodhi and the University of Washington on “Pandemic Preparedness and Mounting Responses” where esteemed speakers shared their experiences, learning, and insights on the topic. Participants from across the globe are participating in this webinar. So far two sessions have been organized where 80 people via Zoom conferencing and 350 people through Facebook and Twitter live streaming. The objective behind these webinars is to reach out to the maximum number of people to increase awareness and prepare for responses against covid-19. This knowledge exchange will help in bridging the gaps and challenges while also helping in learning through other country’s experiences.
